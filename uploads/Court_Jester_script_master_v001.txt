Court Jester

1. EXT. KINGDOM OF VALORIA – SUNRISE 
The sun rises over a sprawling medieval harbor city. Stone walls stretch along the coastline while towering castle spires rise above the town. 
Markets begin to open. Merchants roll carts through narrow streets. Musicians tune instruments. Ships glide across the water toward the busy harbor. The KINGDOM OF VALORIA wakes like a stage preparing for its audience. 

2. EXT. VALORIA DOCKS – MORNING 
Passengers spill from a merchant ship onto crowded stone docks. Among them is ELIAS REED (late 20s), sharp-eyed and curious, carrying everything he owns in a worn travel bag. He pauses and stares up at the massive city before him.  
ELIAS 
Wow.

The harbor roars with energy around him. Guards march past. Merchants shout prices. Sailors unload cargo. Elias smiles like a man who just arrived at the center of the world. 

3. EXT. MARKET SQUARE – DAY 
A sprawling market explodes with life. Street performers juggle flaming torches. Vendors shout over one another. Elias moves through the chaos, fascinated. 

TRAVELER #1 (O.S.) 
They say the king is hosting a banquet tonight. Nobles from every corner of the world. Elias slowly turns toward the castle towers rising above the city. Opportunity flashes across his face. 

4. INT. TAVERN – AFTERNOON 
A loud tavern packed with sailors and travelers. At a table, a DRUNK NOBLE has passed out clutching a sealed invitation.

4A. EXT. MARKET DISTRICT – DAY 
A lively medieval market blooms with color—vendors, dancers, jugglers. In the middle of it all, a BARD leaps onto a table, leading the crowd. 

SONG: "WELCOME TO VALORIA" 
MUSIC BEGINS. 
BARD 
Welcome to Valoria, the jewel beside the sea— 
Where every lord is powerful… and every fool is free! 

TAVERN CROWD 
HEY! 
BARD 
The castle shines with marble towers, the nobles dine for days— 
But half the kingdom’s problems are solved in tavern plays! 
BARD & CROWD 
Welcome to Valoria! Raise your cups and sing! 
Where fortune favors liars— and fools can mock the king! 
SECOND BARD 
The queen is wise and watchful, the king enjoys a laugh— 
But if you cross Lord Cedric… you may not leave by half. 
FULL CROWD 
Welcome to Valoria! Where stories come alive! 
If you can keep your wits about you… you just might survive! 
BARD 
And look— another dreamer who thinks he’ll beat the odds… 
Good luck in Valoria, stranger. You’re walking with the gods. 

Elias carefully slides the invitation closer and studies it. He pulls out parchment and begins copying the design. 
ELIAS 
(to himself) 
Nobles. How hard can it be? He practices an exaggerated noble bow to an imaginary audience. 

5. EXT. CASTLE GATES – EVENING 
Carriages arrive at the enormous castle gates as guards inspect invitations. 
Elias approaches wearing borrowed noble clothing that almost fits. 
GUARD 
Invitation. 

Elias hands over the forged parchment, forcing a confident smile. 
ELIAS 
Lovely evening for a banquet, wouldn't you say? 

The guard studies the invitation, then hands it back. 
GUARD 
Enjoy the festivities. 

Elias exhales quietly and slips through the gates. 

6. INT. ROYAL COURT – NIGHT 
A breathtaking hall filled with nobles, chandeliers, and music. KING AUGUSTUS III sits on an extravagant throne, amused by the spectacle around him. Nearby stands LADY ISABELLA MOREAU, observant and composed. Above the court, LORD CEDRIC BLACKWELL watches from a shadowed balcony. 
Elias enters the hall trying very hard to look like he belongs. Cedric notices him immediately. 
CEDRIC BLACKWELL
(quietly) 
Interesting. 
Elias grabs a drink from a passing tray and nearly collides with Isabella. 
ELIAS 
My apologies. 
ISABELLA 
You're not from Valoria, are you? 
ELIAS 
What gave it away? 

7. INT. ROYAL COURT – CONTINUOUS 
Nobles whisper as Elias stands before the throne. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
A storyteller, you say? That is refreshing. Most of my court only tells boring truths. Laughter ripples through the room. 
CEDRIC 
Your Majesty, I fear our guest may not be exactly who he claims to be. The room quiets. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
Even better. A mystery! 
The king bursts into genuine laughter. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
Our last jester was dreadful. Perhaps fate has delivered us a new one. 
The court erupts with laughter. Elias blinks in disbelief. Cedric does not laugh. 

8. INT. CASTLE CORRIDOR – LATER 
Servants rush Elias through a maze of corridors into a cluttered dressing chamber. A bright jester costume waits on a table. 
SERVANT 
Congratulations. 
ELIAS 
For what? 
SERVANT 
You are now the royal court jester. 
They shove the costume into his arms and leave. 

9. INT. JESTER DRESSING ROOM – CONTINUOUS 
Elias stares at the costume in horror. 
ELIAS 
This cannot be 
happening. 
FINN (O.S.) 
Oh it is definitely happening. 

ELIAS turns to see FINN (early 20s), energetic, wearing the uniform of a castle runner. 
FINN 
I run messages for half the castle. Which means I know two things. 
ELIAS 
And those are? 
FINN 
One, you're not a noble. Two, you're in serious trouble. 
10. INT. SECRET TUNNEL – NIGHT 
Finn leads Elias into a narrow stone tunnel beneath the castle. Torchlight flickers along ancient walls. 
ELIAS
There are tunnels under the castle? 
FINN 
Dozens. Runners use them. Spies probably use them too. 
Finn grins. 
FINN 
Congratulations. You're now the most interesting fool in Valoria.
11. INT. ROYAL COURT – DAY 
The court gathers again the following day. Nobles whisper with anticipation as the king settles onto his throne. Elias stands awkwardly in full jester costume. Bells hang from his hat and sleeves. The outfit jingles every time he moves. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
Well then! Let us see what our new entertainer can do. 
The nobles lean forward eagerly. Elias swallows hard. 
ELIAS 
Right. Of course. Entertainment. That is... why I am dressed like this. 
Scattered laughter. Not encouraging. Elias paces nervously, searching for an idea. 
ELIAS 
Did you ever notice how nobles walk into a room like they own 
the building? 
A few nobles exchange looks. 
ELIAS 
Which is impressive considering most of them can't find the door without 
three servants helping. 
A few nobles snicker. Others glare. The king bursts into laughter. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
Ha! That one was for you, Cedric.
Cedric does not react. 
Elias gains confidence as the king laughs. 
ELIAS 
And the royal advisors—oh they are even better. 
Elias imitates a pompous noble bowing repeatedly. 
ELIAS 
Your Majesty, I bring you important news... which I will explain very slowly so it sounds more impressive. 
The room erupts with laughter. Even Isabella smiles. Cedric watches Elias with increasing concern. 
13. INT. ROYAL COURT BALCONY – LATER 
Cedric stands above the hall watching the performance continue below. A court aide approaches quietly. 
COURT AIDE 
Your Lordship? 
CEDRIC 
Find out everything about this Elias Reed. 
COURT AIDE 
Everything? 
CEDRIC 
If he has secrets... I want them before the king decides he likes this fool too much. 
14. INT. SECRET TUNNEL – NIGHT
Finn leads Elias through another section of underground corridors. They walk beside a narrow channel of water that runs beneath the castle foundations. 
ELIAS 
I thought being a jester meant telling jokes. 
FINN 
It does. 
ELIAS 
Then why does it feel like I 
joined a war? 
FINN 
Because in this castle... jokes can start one. 
15. INT. CASTLE COURTYARD – MORNING 
The sun rises over the castle courtyard. Guards train with swords while servants rush through morning duties. Elias watches from the edge of the courtyard, still wearing pieces of his jester costume. 
ELIAS 
You know... yesterday I was just a traveler. 
FINN 
And today you're the most talked-about person in the castle. 
ELIAS 
That doesn't sound comforting. 
FINN 
It shouldn't. 
They both look up toward the tower where Cedric watches the courtyard from a window above. The game inside Valoria has officially begun.
16. EXT. ROYAL COURTYARD – DAY 
Colorful banners hang across the castle courtyard. Musicians play. Citizens fill the square as nobles watch from balconies. The KING'S FESTIVAL is underway. At the center stands a small stage prepared for entertainers. 
Elias waits nervously backstage in his jester outfit. 
ELIAS 
Remind me why I'm doing this again? 
FINN 
Because the king asked. And when the King asks, people usually prefer to keep 
their heads. 
Elias peeks at the massive crowd. 
ELIAS 
That is a lot of people who could potentially hate me. 
FINN 
Look at it this way — if they hate you, you'll at least be famous. 
17. EXT. FESTIVAL STAGE – MOMENTS LATER 
Trumpets sound. The king gestures toward the stage. Elias steps forward. Bells jingle with every step. The crowd leans in and quiets with curiosity. Nobles sit in velvet chairs. The king watches, amused. Elias steps onto the stage. 
ELIAS 
Good people of Valoria! I have spent only three days in your beautiful lands.
SONG: "THE JESTER'S ROAST" 
MUSIC BEGINS. 
ELIAS 
Good people of Valoria— I bring you joyful news— 
I’ve spent three days among your lords… and I’m still not sure what they do. 
CROWD 
HEY! 
ELIAS 
The farmers work from sunrise, the builders sweat all day— 
The merchants haul their goods to town… and nobles watch and pray. 
One lord counts his silver, another counts his land— 
One more counts the number of rings he’s wearing on his hand! 
But none of them count problems or try to make things right— 
They only count how many forks they used at dinner last night! 
CROWD 
OOOHHH! 
ELIAS 
And Lord Cedric— watching carefully from balconies above— 
If suspicion were a currency… he’d own the crown and glove.
CUT TO:
17A. INT. CASTLE TUNNEL – NIGHT 
Finn pauses in the flickering torchlight after Elias disappears ahead. 
FINN 
You spend your whole life running messages for powerful people… and one day you realize something strange. None of them actually know what’s happening in their own castle. The cooks know. The guards know. The runners know. But kings? Kings only hear what the loudest voice tells them. Kingdom... 
He pauses, studying the nobles watching from above. 
Back To Scene:
ELIAS 
...which is just enough time to realize something remarkable. 
The crowd leans in. 
ELIAS 
The people of Valoria work very hard. The farmers, the merchants, the 
builders... 
He gestures toward the castle balconies. 
ELIAS 
...and the nobles work extremely hard watching them do it. 
The crowd bursts into laughter. Even the king nearly spills his drink laughing. Several nobles look offended. Elias gains confidence as the laughter grows. 
ELIAS 
Take Lord Cedric for example. I have watched him carefully for two days. 
Cedric stands stiffly on the balcony above. 
ELIAS 
I believe his greatest talent is staring suspiciously at everyone in the room until they confess to crimes they didn't commit. 
The crowd ROARS. The king laughs louder than anyone. Cedric does not move. His eyes remain fixed on Elias. 
19. EXT. FESTIVAL CROWD – LATER 
Children mimic Elias' gestures. Merchants repeat his jokes. The performance has turned into a celebration. Finn watches proudly from the edge of the stage. Isabella approaches him quietly. 
ISABELLA 
Your friend is either very brave... or very foolish. 
FINN 
Both, I think. 
Isabella smiles as Elias continues entertaining the crowd. 
20. EXT. CASTLE BALCONY – SAME TIME 
Cedric watches the celebration from above, expression darkening. A court aide steps beside him. 
COURT AIDE 
The people seem to like him. 
CEDRIC 
Yes. That is precisely the problem. 
Cedric turns away from the balcony, already planning his next move.
21. INT. ROYAL COURT – DAY 
The court gathers again, but the atmosphere is tense. Cedric stands beside the throne speaking quietly to the king while guards escort Elias into the center of the hall. Nobles whisper as Elias looks around, confused. 
ELIAS 
I feel like I'm about to regret something I haven't done yet. 
CEDRIC 
Your Majesty, it pains me to report troubling information about our newest 
entertainer. 
Cedric produces a parchment and hands it to the king. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
Troubling? That is disappointing. I was just beginning to enjoy him. Cedric addresses the entire court. 
CEDRIC 
It appears Elias Reed may not be merely a fool, but a spy attempting to gain 
influence in our court. 
Gasps ripple across the room. 
Finn watches from the edge of the chamber, alarmed. 
ELIAS
Spy? I can barely organize my own breakfast. 
Some nobles laugh nervously. Cedric does not. 
22. INT. ROYAL COURT – MOMENTS LATER 
The king studies Elias carefully. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
Tell me honestly, Elias... are you a spy? 
Elias considers the question seriously. 
ELIAS 
Your Majesty, if I were a 
spy, I suspect I would be doing a very poor job of it. 
BEAT: 
The king laughs loudly again. The tension breaks slightly, but Cedric's expression darkens. 
23. INT. CASTLE CORRIDOR – LATER 
Guards escort Elias out of the court. Finn rushes up beside him. 
FINN 
Cedric planted something, didn't he? 
ELIAS 
That seems to be the pattern around here. 
The guards push Elias forward through the corridor. 
24. INT. QUIET CASTLE CHAMBER – NIGHT 
Elias sits alone at a long table under dim candlelight. A door creaks open. A tall, composed man enters: THOMAS WREN. He studies Elias calmly before sitting across from him. 
WREN
You have caused quite a disturbance in a very short 
amount of time. 
ELIAS 
I am beginning to notice that. 
WREN 
Cedric believes you are dangerous. 
ELIAS 
Cedric believes a lot of things about me. Most of them incorrect. 
25. INT. CHAMBER – CONTINUOUS 
Wren leans forward slightly. 
WREN 
The question is not whether Cedric is correct. 
WREN 
The question is whether you might become dangerous in the right circumstances. 
Elias studies him carefully. 
ELIAS 
That sounds like the beginning of a job offer. 
A faint smile appears on Wren's face. 
WREN 
Let us call it an opportunity.
26. INT. QUIET CASTLE CHAMBER – NIGHT 
Wren pours two small cups of wine. The candlelight flickers across the stone walls. Elias watches him carefully, unsure what game is being played. 
ELIAS 
If this is where you tell me I'm secretly working for you now, I'd appreciate 
some warning. 
Wren slides a cup toward Elias. 
WREN 
Tell me something, Elias. What does a jester do better than anyone in the 
kingdom? 
ELIAS 
Embarrass himself publicly? 
A faint smile from Wren. 
WREN 
A fool hears everything. Nobles speak freely around someone they believe 
harmless. 
Elias leans back, realizing where this is going. 
ELIAS 
You want me to listen. 
27. INT. CHAMBER – CONTINUOUS 
WREN 
Cedric is building influence quietly. 
WREN 
Trade deals. Military alliances. Loyalists in the court. 
He studies Elias carefully. 
WREN 
But he cannot hide his plans from a room if someone in that room hears 
everything. 
ELIAS 
And you think that someone is the man wearing bells on his hat. 
WREN 
Precisely. 
28. INT. CASTLE CORRIDOR – SAME NIGHT 
Finn waits outside the chamber door nervously. Elias steps out moments later. 
FINN 
So? Are we in trouble? 
ELIAS 
Apparently I'm being promoted from court fool to accidental spy. 
FINN 
That sounds worse. 
ELIAS 
Much worse. 
29. INT. CASTLE BALCONY – NIGHT 
Cedric stands overlooking the quiet courtyard below. A messenger approaches with a sealed document. 
MESSENGER 
Your Lordship. The northern alliance has agreed. Cedric breaks the seal and reads the letter. A cold smile spreads across his face. 
CEDRIC 
Good. Everything is moving exactly as planned.
30. INT. CASTLE TUNNELS – NIGHT 
Torchlight flickers across damp stone walls. Finn leads Elias through a maze of narrow underground passages beneath the castle. Water drips from the ceiling. The distant echo of guards marching above vibrates through the stone. Elias struggles to keep up in his jester outfit. 
ELIAS 
I would like to officially say this is the strangest job I have ever had. 
FINN 
You said that yesterday. 
ELIAS 
Yesterday I wasn't underground in a tunnel that smells like wet socks. 
31. INT. TUNNEL CHAMBER – CONTINUOUS 
Finn stops suddenly at a hidden stone alcove carved into the wall. 
He brushes dust away from a wooden panel partially hidden behind loose stones. He pulls it open revealing a narrow storage cavity inside the wall. 
ELIAS 
Please tell me that isn't where the spiders live. Finn pulls out a rolled parchment sealed with dark wax. 
FINN 
This isn't normal. Nobody stores documents down here. 
32. INT. TUNNEL CHAMBER – MOMENTS LATER
Finn spreads the parchment across a crate. Elias leans over his shoulder. 
It is a detailed MAP of the surrounding kingdoms and trade routes. 
Red markings circle multiple military positions near Valoria's northern border. 
ELIAS 
That... doesn't look like a festival planning map. 
FINN 
Those are supply routes. And those symbols... 
Finn traces a line connecting several locations. 
FINN 
They're troop staging areas. 
ELIAS 
Troops for who? 
They both slowly realize the answer. 
FINN 
Cedric. 
33. INT. TUNNEL CHAMBER – CONTINUOUS 
Elias studies the map carefully, suddenly very serious. This is the first time we see him thinking strategically rather than joking. 
ELIAS 
If those troops move... they could surround the capital. 
FINN 
That's not politics. That's a coup. 
ELIAS
And somehow the court jester found the invasion plans. 
A distant sound echoes through the tunnels. Boots approaching. Guards. 
34. INT. TUNNEL PASSAGE – MOMENTS LATER 
Finn quickly rolls up the map. He blows out the torch and grabs Elias's arm. They press themselves into a dark side passage as TWO GUARDS walk past carrying lanterns. 
GUARD #1 
Lord Cedric wants the tunnels checked twice tonight. 
GUARD #2 
After the festival embarrassment, I don't blame him. The guards disappear down the corridor. 
Finn exhales slowly. 
ELIAS 
Good news. I think we just found the most dangerous secret in the kingdom. 
FINN 
Bad news? 
ELIAS 
We're the only ones who know it.
CUT TO:
34A. EXT. CASTLE BALCONY – NIGHT 
Isabella watches the crowd laughing below as Elias performs. 
ISABELLA 
Courts are strange places. Everyone speaks carefully… everyone bows…everyone agrees. And somehow in all that politeness… the truth disappears. 
Yet tonight… a fool walks into the court and says the one thing no noble dares.
CUT TO:
35. INT. ROYAL COURT – DAY 
The court gathers once again. Nobles whisper as Elias enters with exaggerated confidence. Cedric stands beside the KING advising him quietly. Finn watches nervously from the edge of the chamber. Elias approaches the throne and bows clumsily. Bells jingle loudly. 
ELIAS 
Your Majesty, I have prepared a new performance today. A story about loyalty. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
A story? I do enjoy stories. Proceed. 
36. INT. ROYAL COURT – CONTINUOUS 
Elias begins pacing across the floor like a theatrical storyteller. 
ELIAS 
Once upon a time there was a very loyal advisor who served his king 
faithfully... 
Cedric watches him with mild curiosity. 
ELIAS 
But secretly this advisor moved soldiers across the countryside. Quiet little armies hiding along trade roads and northern borders. Several nobles shift uncomfortably. Cedric’s expression tightens slightly.
37. INT. ROYAL COURT – MOMENTS LATER 
Elias continues, gaining energy as the court listens. 
ELIAS 
And this advisor thought no one would notice because, after all, who 
listens to the fool in the room? 
Elias suddenly stops and looks directly at Cedric. The room grows quiet. 
But 
ELIAS 
sometimes... the fool hears everything. 
38. INT. ROYAL COURT – CONTINUOUS 
The KING studies Elias carefully now. For a brief moment the humor disappears from his face. He understands. But just as quickly, he bursts into loud laughter. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
Ha! Wonderful! A suspicious advisor with imaginary armies! Very clever! 
The court relaxes and laughs again. Cedric forces a polite smile, but his eyes remain fixed on Elias. 
39. INT. ROYAL COURT – LATER 
The performance ends and nobles disperse across the chamber. Elias approaches the throne quietly as others leave. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
You are either the bravest fool I have ever met...
The king leans slightly closer. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
...or the most useful. 
ELIAS 
I prefer useful. 
The king glances briefly toward Cedric across the room, then back to Elias. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
Then continue listening, jester. 
Elias nods slowly. Across the chamber Cedric watches them both, realizing something has changed.
40. INT. ROYAL COURT – MORNING 
The court gathers again. Cedric stands before the throne holding a sealed scroll. 
Elias stands nearby trying to look relaxed, though Finn watches Cedric suspiciously. 
CEDRIC 
Your Majesty, an opportunity has presented itself. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
Opportunities are usually expensive. Continue. 
CEDRIC 
Our neighbors in North Valen request a diplomatic representative for their spring 
council. 
CEDRIC 
A gesture of goodwill between kingdoms. 
Cedric glances subtly toward Elias. 
CEDRIC 
Perhaps our beloved court jester could attend. A symbol of Valoria's 
humor and goodwill. The nobles murmur. Elias blinks in surprise. 
ELIAS 
I'm sorry... did you just send the jester to diplomacy school? 
KING AUGUSTUS 
It would certainly make the meetings less boring. 
The king studies Cedric carefully — he senses something. 
KING AUGUSTUS 
Very well. Elias shall represent us. 
Cedric bows politely, hiding a small satisfied smile. 
41. INT. CASTLE CORRIDOR – LATER 
Elias and Finn walk quickly down the corridor. Finn looks alarmed. 
FINN 
That was a trap. 
ELIAS 
You think? I thought they were rewarding my excellent diplomacy skills. 
FINN 
Cedric would never send you anywhere helpful. 
ELIAS 
So we're traveling to another kingdom... because the villain suggested it. 
FINN
Yes. 
ELIAS 
That feels bad. 
42. EXT. CASTLE COURTYARD – DAY
A carriage waits near the castle gate. Guards prepare horses while servants load supplies. Elias studies the carriage like it might explode. 
ELIAS 
I miss when my biggest problem was telling bad jokes. 
FINN 
You still do that. Now people just try to kill you afterwards. 
43. EXT. CASTLE BALCONY – SAME TIME 
Cedric watches the courtyard from above. A CAPTAIN OF GUARDS approaches quietly. 
CAPTAIN 
Arrangements have been made. The road through Grey Pass is... dangerous. 
CEDRIC 
Accidents happen in the mountains. 
Cedric watches as Elias climbs into the carriage below. 
CEDRIC 
Especially to fools who travel too far from home.
46. EXT. MOUNTAIN ROAD – GREY PASS – DAY 
A narrow mountain road winds along steep cliffs. The diplomatic carriage climbs slowly through the foggy pass. Two GUARDS ride ahead while Elias and Finn sit inside the carriage surrounded by supplies. 
ELIAS 
I have a bad feeling about this road. 
FINN 
That's because it is the most dangerous road in the kingdom. 
ELIAS 
I miss the castle already. At least there the assassination attempts were 
indoors. 
45. EXT. GREY PASS CHECKPOINT – MOMENTS LATER 
The road bends around a rocky corner revealing a wooden barricade across the path. 
A SMALL CHECKPOINT has been set up with several ARMED MEN wearing guard uniforms. One of them raises a hand to stop the carriage. 
FAKE CAPTAIN 
Halt! Royal checkpoint. Inspection required. 
The carriage stops. Finn peers out cautiously. 
FINN
That checkpoint wasn't here last time I traveled this road. 
ELIAS
That seems suspicious. 
46. EXT. CHECKPOINT – CONTINUOUS 
Elias steps out of the carriage trying to look confident. The fake soldiers slowly surround the road. 
ELIAS
Good afternoon, loyal protectors of the road. We are on a diplomatic 
mission. 
FAKE CAPTAIN 
Of course you are. 
The captain signals subtly. The soldiers tighten their circle. 
FAKE CAPTAIN 
Step away from the carriage. Slowly. 
Finn notices one of the soldiers revealing a hidden dagger instead of a royal insignia. 
FINN 
Elias… they aren't guards. 
ELIAS 
I was afraid you might say that. 
47. EXT. GREY PASS – AMBUSH 
Suddenly the soldiers draw weapons. The two REAL GUARDS riding with the carriage are knocked from their horses. Chaos erupts on the narrow mountain road. 
BANDIT 
Take the fool alive! The boy too! 
Finn grabs Elias and pulls him behind the carriage. 
ELIAS
I knew diplomacy was dangerous! 
FINN 
Run! 
48. EXT. CLIFFSIDE PATH – CONTINUOUS 
Finn drags Elias toward a narrow cliffside trail beside the road. Arrows strike the carriage behind them. 
Elias trips over his own jester bells. 
ELIAS 
These bells are a terrible design flaw! 
Finn shoves Elias forward and they disappear down the steep mountain path as bandits chase after them.
49. EXT. CLIFFSIDE TRAIL – GREY PASS – DAY 
Finn and Elias sprint down a narrow trail carved into the mountainside. 
Bandits chase behind them, shouting and slipping on loose gravel. 
The cliff drops hundreds of feet beside them. 
ELIAS 
I regret every life decision that led to this mountain! 
FINN 
Keep moving! 
50. EXT. MOUNTAIN LEDGE – CONTINUOUS 
The path narrows into a rocky ledge. Elias scrambles forward and accidentally kicks a loose stone. The rock tumbles down the slope, knocking other stones loose. 
ELIAS 
Uh oh. 
The small stones begin sliding faster. 
51. EXT. GREY PASS SLOPE – MOMENTS LATER 
The sliding stones turn into a roaring CASCADE of rock and dirt. 
A MASSIVE ROCKSLIDE thunders down the mountainside. Bandits scatter in panic as the avalanche of stone crashes toward them. 
BANDIT 
Move! Move! 
The rockslide slams into the trail, sweeping bandits off their feet and burying the checkpoint road behind them. 
52. EXT. CLIFFSIDE – CONTINUOUS 
Finn grabs Elias and pulls him behind a large boulder as rocks crash past. Dust fills the air. The thunder of falling stone slowly fades. 
Silence returns to the mountains. 
ELIAS 
Did... did I just destroy a mountain?
FINN 
Technically you only loosened one rock. The mountain did the rest. 
53. EXT. FOREST EDGE BELOW GREY PASS – LATER 
Finn and Elias emerge from the base of the pass into a dense green forest. The distant mountain road is now completely buried by the rockslide. They both stare back at the destruction. 
ELIAS 
Good news. I think we survived the trap. 
FINN 
Bad news. We're now in the wilderness with no horses, no supplies... 
ELIAS 
And probably still being hunted. 
Finn sighs. 
FINN 
Yes. That too.
53A. EXT. MOUNTAIN SLOPE – DAY 
Finn looks back at the destroyed mountain road after the rockslide. 
FINN 
Do you know what the strange thing is about heroes? Nobody decides to be one in the morning. One moment you’re delivering messages… staying out of
