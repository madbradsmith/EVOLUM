NOT TODAY!
Written by
MadBrad Smith


FADE IN:


BLACK.

Industrial ambience.

Wind through chain link.

Machinery humming.

Then—

BANG!


EXT. FACTORY PARKING LOT – NIGHT

A heavy industrial door slams open.

MALIK TURNER (early 30s) steps into the night.

Construction vest. Work boots. Helmet in hand.

End of a long shift.

He rolls his neck.

Cracks his shoulder.

The lot is dimly lit. Sodium lights. Long shadows.

Malik walks toward camera as it slowly tracks backward.

He passes frame.

CUT.


INT. MALIK’S SUV – NIGHT

Malik tosses the helmet aside and starts the engine.

A podcast is already playing.

                      HOST (V.O.)
          You’d be surprised how much illegal
          activity happens in rideshare vehicles.

Malik glances in the rearview mirror.

Empty back seat.

Then he turns to check behind him.

Still empty.

He nods.

He pulls out his phone.

Four notifications.

Two voicemails. Two texts.

He plays the voicemail.

                      WOMAN (V.O.)
          Mr. Turner, if you do not arrive before
          nine PM the rental assistance will be
          given to the next applicant.

Malik exhales.

Next voicemail.

                      LANDLORD (V.O.)
          Malik, I need that rent handled.

Malik rubs his face.

Checks texts.

BABY MAMA: "Can you cashapp me $200?"

SON: "You coming tomorrow? Coach said I might start."

Malik stares at the message.

Then sets the phone down.

He wipes his face with body wipes.

Changes shirts.

Puts on a rideshare jacket.

Podcast clicks off.

Soft music replaces it.

He drives.


EXT. COFFEE SHOP – NIGHT

Malik pulls up.

Checks rideshare app.

Nothing.

He heads inside.


INT. COFFEE SHOP – NIGHT

Malik pours coffee.

Glances at the rideshare app repeatedly.

A customer ahead of him talks loudly.

                      CUSTOMER
          Man I had a rideshare driver fall
          asleep at a red light.

The cashier laughs.

                      CUSTOMER
          I had to wake him up!

Malik smirks.

Pays.

Leaves.


INT. MALIK’S SUV – NIGHT

Malik waits.

Coffee in hand.

Watching the rideshare app.

PING.

Ride request.

He accepts.

The night begins.


EXT. PICKUP – NIGHT

RACHEL and RICO enter the car.

Rico places a long hard-shell case beside him.

Malik notices it in the mirror.

Looks away.

Looks back.

Double take.

Quick glance over his shoulder.

Rico doesn't notice.

BEEP.

Malik’s phone lights up.

NEW STOP ADDED.

Rachel never looks up from her phone.

Malik drives.


INT. SUV – MOVING – NIGHT

More passengers join.

TIFFANY.

Then DEV.

Conversation grows.

Personal stories.

                      DEV
          I might propose next month.

                      TIFFANY
          Work is killing me lately.

Rico quietly identifies plants outside.

Malik watches them through the mirror.


EXT. GAS STATION – NIGHT

Dev needs the bathroom.

Malik pulls in.

Dev runs inside.

Rachel and Tiffany whisper.

Malik studies the mirror.

Suspicion building.


LATER – THE REVEAL

Malik realizes the passengers are organizing a fundraiser.

The stops were supply pickups.

The case holds gardening tools.

Malik has misread everything.


EXT. BABY MAMA’S APARTMENT – NIGHT

Malik arrives.

His son waits in the window.

Malik walks up with a bag of food.

The son opens the door.

                      SON
          Love you Dad.

They hug.

The son runs inside.

The mother appears.

                      MOTHER
          Hey.

                      MALIK
          Hey.

She starts closing the door.

Malik stops it.

Hands her $200.

She looks at the money.

                      MOTHER
          Thank you.

Door closes.


INT. MALIK’S SUV – NIGHT

Malik drives away.

Music low.

He mouths the words.

The chorus hits.

Now he sings.

He pulls up to a stop sign.

Takes off his jacket.

Throws it in the passenger seat.

People on the corner hear the music.

They vibe with it.

The light turns green.

Malik drives through the night.

Still singing.

Chicago lights sliding past.

FADE OUT.
