***Who Invited You?

Logline

Two broke best friends discover they can eat for free by crashing strangers’ backyard cookouts, but when their hustle spirals into a citywide summer adventure of increasingly outrageous BBQ infiltrations, they must pull off one final finesse before their luck — and the police — run out.

Short Story Summary

Deuce and Lil Kev are two broke best friends navigating life on the streets of Chicago. One summer day, on a random dare, they discover something incredible:

If you walk into a cookout like you belong there… nobody questions you.

What starts as a joke quickly becomes their new survival strategy as the pair spend the entire Fourth of July weekend crashing backyard BBQs across the city.

But every cookout has different rules, different families, and different problems.

As the weekend spirals into chaos, Deuce and Lil Kev must survive suspicious aunties, territorial grill masters, nosy neighbors, and eventually the police — all while trying to bring food back to their homeless community.

Their final stop?

A massive Latin family cookout that might be their biggest finesse yet… or the moment everything falls apart.

⸻

Long Story Summary

Deuce and Lil Kev are two lifelong friends who know how to survive with nothing but charm, confidence, and quick thinking.

One afternoon, while wandering through a Chicago neighborhood during the summer, they stumble upon a backyard cookout.

On a dare, they walk in pretending they were invited.

No one questions them.

They eat like kings.

From that moment on, they realize something powerful:

If you act like you belong… people believe you.

Soon the pair develop a system — The Cookout Rules — a set of guidelines for infiltrating backyard BBQs without getting caught.

Over the course of a chaotic Fourth of July weekend, they travel across Chicago crashing cookouts of every kind: block parties, church picnics, suburban BBQs, family reunions, and more.

But each new cookout becomes more complicated.

Suspicious relatives begin asking questions. Rival party crashers appear. Neighborhoods start recognizing them.

And as their reputation spreads, so do the risks.

Through it all, Deuce and Lil Kev continue bringing food back to their homeless friends, turning their ridiculous hustle into something surprisingly meaningful.

But their biggest challenge comes at the final cookout — a massive Latin family BBQ where Lil Kev convinces the oldest uncle they’re related.

Everything goes perfectly…

Until the police arrive.

When officers ask the confused uncle if he knows Lil Kev, he proudly confirms:

“Of course. He’s family.”

Lil Kev celebrates.

Then Deuce whispers the truth.

The uncle has dementia.

The two friends walk away victorious, laughing, plates full, and already planning their next cookout.

The World of the Film

Who Invited You? takes place over one chaotic Fourth of July weekend in Chicago, when the entire city seems to be grilling something.

Backyards, parks, alleys, rooftops, and vacant lots transform into cookout zones filled with music, laughter, smoke from grills, and the smell of barbecue drifting through the neighborhoods.

The city becomes a playground for Deuce and Lil Kev as they move from cookout to cookout, navigating different communities, personalities, and social dynamics.

Every yard feels like a different world with its own rules, traditions, and characters.

The tone blends:

• street-level comedy
• summer adventure
• community warmth
• improvisational humor

Visually, the movie is vibrant and alive — full of color, music, and the energy of summer in Chicago.

⸻

Chicago Summer Cookout Culture

Chicago cookouts are more than just meals — they’re social events where families, friends, and neighbors come together.

Elements include:

• grills smoking on every block
• folding chairs and card tables
• coolers packed with drinks
• domino games and card games
• music blasting from speakers
• kids running through sprinklers

For Deuce and Lil Kev, every cookout becomes a new challenge.

Each one has:

• a different family dynamic
• a different level of suspicion
• a different opportunity to finesse a plate.

The Goodwill Thrift Store Stop

Before starting their weekend cookout hustle, Deuce and Lil Kev realize they need to look the part.

They stop at a neighborhood Goodwill thrift store to build their disguises.

The store becomes their unofficial costume department.

Montage moment:

They try on outfits in front of the mirror.

Examples:

• suburban dad outfits
• church picnic clothes
• sports jerseys
• khaki shorts and polos
• oversized hats
• random family reunion T-shirts

Lil Kev holds up a ridiculous shirt.

“This say ‘Johnson Family Reunion 2004.’”

Deuce shrugs.

“That mean we cousins now.”

Throughout the movie, they keep returning to the thrift store to upgrade their disguises depending on the type of cookout they’re crashing.

The Goodwill clerk slowly becomes suspicious as he sees them repeatedly buying clothes for increasingly strange reasons.

The Cookout Rules (Recurring Comedy)

Deuce and Lil Kev develop a playbook they repeat throughout the film.

Rule #1 — Find the oldest person first.
If the elder thinks you belong, everyone else assumes you do.

Rule #2 — Always bring something.
Even if it’s just a bag of ice.

Rule #3 — Compliment the grill master immediately.

Rule #4 — Never rush the food table.

Rule #5 — Find the drunk uncle.

These rules appear throughout the movie, sometimes helping them succeed… and sometimes failing spectacularly.

Main Characters

Deuce (Early 40s)

Deuce is smooth, confident, and believes he’s the smartest guy in every room.

Even though he’s in his early 40s, Deuce still behaves like a teenager. He refuses to fully grow up and approaches life like it’s one long hustle.

He carries himself with the confidence of someone who believes presentation is everything. If you walk into a place like you belong there, Deuce believes people will accept it.

Deuce is the strategist of the duo, the one who created most of the “Cookout Rules.” He studies the layout of every backyard like a tactical operation:
	•	Who’s the oldest person?
	•	Who’s running the grill?
	•	Where’s the food table?

Deuce tries to keep their cookout hustle organized and low-profile, but his plans constantly get derailed by Lil Kev’s unpredictable behavior.

Despite all the schemes, Deuce has a good heart. He makes sure they bring food back to their homeless community whenever they score big at a cookout.

Character energy:
Smooth talker. Street philosopher. Forever young in his own mind.

⸻

Lil Kev (Early 40s)

Lil Kev is loud, lovable, and completely unpredictable.

Despite being in his early 40s, he still behaves like an excited teenager. He’s impulsive, constantly talking, and often blurts out ridiculous lies that Deuce then has to fix.

Lil Kev is overweight — which is why everyone ironically calls him “Lil Kev.”

But what really makes him special is his superhuman sense of smell for barbecue.

Lil Kev can literally sniff out a cookout from blocks away.

He’ll suddenly stop walking, lift his head like a bloodhound, and say something like:

“Hold up… ribs.”

Then he starts following the scent through the neighborhood until they find a backyard full of grills.

This ability becomes the unofficial navigation system for their entire operation.

Lil Kev may cause chaos, but he’s also the heart of the duo. His love for food, people, and life makes him instantly likable to strangers — which is exactly how they keep getting into cookouts.

Character energy:
Human bloodhound. Walking appetite. Chaos machine.

⸻

Running Gag (Lil Kev’s BBQ Radar)

Throughout the movie, Lil Kev’s nose leads them to cookouts.

Examples:

Walking down the street…

Lil Kev suddenly stops.

Sniffs the air.

“Brisket.”

Deuce looks confused.

“You sure?”

Lil Kev starts walking.

“Follow me.”

They turn the corner and see a massive cookout.

Deuce shakes his head.

“I swear your nose got GPS.”

Small Comedic Detail That Makes It Even Better

Lil Kev can even identify the type of meat.

Examples:

“Chicken.”

Deuce:

“How you smell burnt ribs from two blocks away?”

Lil Kev:

“Because they burnt.”

The Cookouts of Chicago

A Summer BBQ Adventure

Over one Fourth of July weekend, Deuce and Lil Kev travel across Chicago crashing cookouts of every kind. Each location introduces new characters, new problems, and new opportunities for their hustle.

The city becomes a map of food, music, and chaos.

⸻

Cookout #1

The Neighborhood Block Party

This is where the hustle really begins.

A residential street filled with grills, folding chairs, and kids running through sprinklers.

Music blasting from car speakers.

Domino tables everywhere.

Lil Kev smells ribs from three blocks away and leads Deuce straight to the party.

They blend in easily because the event is chaotic and full of people coming and going.

Obstacle:

The Suspicious Auntie who keeps asking:

“Who invited y’all?”

Deuce smooth-talks her by complimenting her potato salad.

⸻

Cookout #2

The Church Picnic

A daytime cookout in a church parking lot.

White tents, long folding tables, and homemade dishes everywhere.

Everything feels polite… but heavily monitored.

Obstacle:

The Church Deacon who seems to know everyone and starts asking questions.

Deuce and Lil Kev must pretend they’re distant cousins visiting from out of town.

Lil Kev accidentally eats six plates before Deuce reminds him to slow down.

⸻

Cookout #3

The Suburban Backyard BBQ

A quiet suburban neighborhood where everything is perfectly organized.

Matching patio furniture.

Carefully arranged food tables.

A grill master wearing a chef apron.

Obstacle:

The host keeps introducing them to people expecting them to know the family.

Lil Kev invents a ridiculous fake backstory about growing up with the host.

The lie gets bigger and bigger.

⸻

Cookout #4

The Park Cookout

Huge gathering at a public park.

Dozens of grills.

Multiple families cooking at once.

Music competing from different speakers.

This is the easiest place to blend in.

Obstacle:

Another pair of cookout crashers appears.

Now Deuce and Lil Kev must compete for food territory.

⸻

Cookout #5

The College Graduation BBQ

Young crowd celebrating a recent graduate.

Music loud.

People dancing.

Beer everywhere.

Lil Kev immediately fits in because nobody is paying attention to who belongs there.

Obstacle:

Someone asks Deuce to give a toast to the graduate.

He has no idea who the graduate is.

⸻

Cookout #6

The Rooftop Cookout

Urban rooftop party overlooking the city.

Small but stylish crowd.

Fancy grills and craft cocktails.

Deuce and Lil Kev are clearly out of place.

Obstacle:

Security at the building entrance.

They must bluff their way upstairs.

⸻

Final Cookout

The Massive Latin Family BBQ

This is the biggest cookout in the movie.

A huge backyard filled with relatives.

Music blasting.

Kids running everywhere.

Multiple grills cooking:
	•	carne asada
	•	chicken
	•	ribs
	•	corn

Lil Kev convinces the oldest uncle they are family.

Everything is perfect.

They are eating like kings.

Then…

Police show up.

The uncle is asked:

“Do you know him?”

The uncle proudly says:

“Yes… he’s family.”

Lil Kev celebrates.

Deuce leans over and whispers:

“He got dementia.”
