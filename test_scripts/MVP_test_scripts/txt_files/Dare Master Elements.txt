***DARE MASTER

Vertical Series Show Bible

Tagline: Any Dare. Any Time.

⸻

SERIES OVERVIEW

Format: Vertical social series (9:16)
Episode Length: 3–6 minutes
Season Length: 40 episodes
Genre: Comedy / Social Experiment / Teen Adventure

Tone:
Energetic, chaotic, funny, and authentic.

Think the energy of:
	•	TikTok street content
	•	YouTube challenge channels
	•	Teen comedy shows

But with a story-driven cast and recurring characters.

⸻

SERIES PREMISE

When a fearless teenage girl starts completing outrageous dares suggested by her followers, her account Dare Master quickly goes viral.

Every episode, Maya Cruz accepts a new challenge submitted by the internet.

The only rule:

She must complete the dare in public.

As the dares grow bigger, her friend group becomes part of the chaos—sometimes helping, sometimes sabotaging, and sometimes becoming the victims of the dare themselves.

The audience doesn’t just watch the show.

They create it.

⸻

THE WORLD

The show takes place primarily in real-world environments:

• shopping malls
• parks
• schools
• city streets
• festivals
• restaurants
• public transportation

Anywhere the public might appear.

Each episode feels like a live social experiment.

The unpredictability of strangers creates most of the comedy.

⸻

THE VISUAL STYLE

The series is filmed entirely vertical to match social media platforms.

Visual elements include:

• on-screen comments
• reaction emojis
• follower polls
• countdown timers
• dare counters

Editing is fast and energetic.

Jump cuts, reaction shots, and graphics mimic the language of social media.

⸻

THE DARE MASTER

Maya Cruz

Age: 16–17

Maya is fearless, quick-witted, and thrives in awkward situations.

She created the Dare Master account with her friends as a joke, but when one of her videos unexpectedly goes viral, she becomes determined to prove she can complete any dare the internet throws at her.

Her personality combines:

• confidence
• humor
• stubborn determination

She never backs down from a challenge.

⸻

SIGNATURE STYLE

Maya is a tomboy who mostly wears thrift-store clothes.

Oversized flannels.
Vintage jackets.
Cargo pants.

But there’s one thing she takes extremely seriously.

Her sneakers.

Maya is a Jordan sneaker collector and refuses to damage them no matter how ridiculous the dare becomes.

Her shoes become a recurring visual gag.

⸻

THE DARE RULES

To keep the show structured:
	1.	The dare must be legal
	2.	The dare must happen in public
	3.	The dare must be completed on camera
	4.	If she fails, she must perform a punishment dare

⸻

Official Punishment Dare List

These punishments are triggered whenever Maya fails a challenge.

⸻

1. The Embarrassing Dance

Maya must perform a ridiculous dance for 30 seconds in public.

Examples:

• chicken dance
• robot dance
• worm attempt

Marcus usually supplies the music.

⸻

2. Let Lina Dress Her

Lina gets to choose Maya’s outfit for the next episode.

Outfits could include:

• thrift store chaos
• grandma sweater combo
• mismatched colors

The only rule:

Maya still gets to wear Jordans.

⸻

3. Sing a Song in Public

Maya must sing loudly in public.

Examples:

• Disney song
• pop song
• random improvised song

Jay films from a distance while strangers react.

⸻

4. Random Stranger Dare

A random person on the street gets to give Maya a mini dare.

Example:

“Spin around three times and shout your favorite pizza topping.”

⸻

5. The Joke Punishment

Maya must tell jokes to strangers until someone laughs.

The problem:

Marcus gives her terrible jokes.

⸻

6. The Backwards Walk

Maya must walk backwards for five minutes in public while talking normally.

This confuses strangers and creates funny interactions.

⸻

7. Marcus’ Revenge Dare

Marcus gets to create a punishment.

This usually goes badly for Maya.

Examples:

• wear a ridiculous hat
• pretend to be a statue
• speak in pirate voice

⸻

8. The Compliment Marathon

Maya must compliment everyone she passes for 10 minutes.

This becomes awkward very quickly.

⸻

9. The Public Karaoke

Marcus blasts music and Maya must sing along in public immediately.

Location examples:

• mall
• park
• bus stop

⸻

10. The Internet Punishment

Followers vote on the punishment in the comments.

This keeps the audience involved.

⸻

Running Gag

Whenever punishment is announced:

Jay zooms the camera to Maya’s Jordans.

Maya immediately checks them.

“Wait… this won’t ruin my shoes, right?”

⸻

Visual Moment

A spray-paint graphic appears on screen:

PUNISHMENT DARE ACTIVATED

This becomes a recognizable moment in the series.

EPISODE STRUCTURE

Each episode follows the same rhythm.

1 — Hook (5 seconds)

The dare appears as a comment.

2 — Setup

Maya explains the challenge.

3 — Attempt

The dare unfolds in public.

4 — Result

Success or failure.

5 — Audience Engagement

Maya asks viewers to submit the next dare.

⸻

THE FRIEND GROUP

The friend group forms the core ensemble of the series.

They help film, plan, sabotage, and react to the dares.

Their personalities balance Maya’s fearless energy.

⸻

DETAILED CHARACTER DESCRIPTIONS

Jay – The Cameraman

Jay is Maya’s best friend and the primary camera operator.

He is sarcastic, observant, and often the voice of reason.

Jay enjoys documenting chaos but rarely participates directly.

His commentary often provides the funniest moments because he reacts to the absurdity happening in front of him.

Jay sometimes secretly suggests dares to make Maya’s life harder.

⸻

Lina – The Skeptic

Lina is the cautious one in the group.

She constantly warns Maya that each new dare is a terrible idea.

Despite her hesitation, Lina always ends up helping.

Her reactions—shock, embarrassment, disbelief—create great comedic contrast.

When a dare becomes too awkward, Lina is usually the one trying to pull Maya out of the situation.

⸻

Marcus – The Hype Man

Marcus lives for chaos.

He encourages the most ridiculous dares and constantly pushes Maya to go bigger.

He’s loud, enthusiastic, and always ready to escalate the situation.

Marcus frequently becomes the target of reverse dares, which usually backfires on him.

⸻

FRIEND GROUP DYNAMIC

The group functions like a small production crew.

Maya – The fearless leader
Jay – The camera and narrator
Lina – The voice of caution
Marcus – The chaos generator

Their chemistry makes the show feel like a real friend group adventure, not just a challenge channel.

⸻

SEASON ARC

Although each episode stands alone, the season gradually escalates.

Early episodes feature small dares.

Later episodes involve:

• bigger crowds
• more ambitious stunts
• dares that include entire groups of strangers

The season builds toward a final audience-voted ultimate dare.

⸻

SEASON FINALE

Followers vote for the most outrageous challenge.

The Dare Master must attempt it live.

Success or failure becomes the climax of the season.

⸻

WHY THE SERIES WORKS

The show blends three powerful elements:

Audience interaction
Teen personality-driven storytelling
Real-world unpredictability

The result is a format that is both entertaining and highly shareable.
