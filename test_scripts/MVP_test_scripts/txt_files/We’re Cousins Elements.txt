*** We’re Cousins?!? Elements

Format: Feature Film
Genre: Romantic Comedy / Family Comedy

Logline

After a year of perfect dating, a happy couple attends a family reunion only to discover they share the same family tree. When they decide their distant relation shouldn’t end their love, they must convince an entire family that sometimes love doesn’t follow the rules.

⸻

Tone

Think a mix of:
	•	Meet the Parents
	•	My Big Fat Greek Wedding
	•	The Proposal

Lots of family chaos, uncomfortable honesty, and heartfelt comedy.

⸻

The Premise

JAMIE and MARCO (names can change) are the perfect couple.

They:
	•	finish each other’s sentences
	•	share the same sense of humor
	•	have been dating for one year

They’re so solid that they decide it’s time to meet the extended families at a huge summer reunion.

Then they notice something strange.

Same last names.
Same hometown roots.
Same relatives…

Then the truth hits.

They’re distant cousins.

⸻

The Central Conflict

The couple decides:

“We’ve already been together a year. This changes nothing.”

But the family loses their minds.

Some reactions:
	•	The aunt who faints
	•	The cousin who starts Googling genetics
	•	The uncle yelling “THIS ISN’T ALABAMA!”
	•	Parents mortified
	•	The family WhatsApp exploding

The reunion turns into a full family intervention.

⸻

The Emotional Core

Late in the film, the Great Grandmother pulls Jamie aside.

She quietly confesses:

She and her husband were also cousins.

Back then it wasn’t unusual in their rural community.

She tells her:

“People act like love is something you can schedule. It isn’t.”

This changes everything.

⸻

The Resolution

Great Grandma stands up at the reunion dinner and tells the story.

The family slowly realizes:
	•	the couple is distantly related
	•	they’ve already been together a year
	•	they clearly love each other

The family finally accepts them.

Someone raises a toast:

“Well… at least we already know they’ll fit in.”

⸻

Tagline Options
	1.	“Love runs in the family.”
	2.	“They found the perfect partner… in the wrong branch of the family tree.”
	3.	“Family reunions just got complicated.”
	4.	“Some relationships are closer than expected.”
	5.	“It’s not weird… it’s just family.”

⸻

Why This Works

This movie works because the tone stays sweet.

The joke isn’t incest —
the joke is family panic and social awkwardness.

And the audience roots for them because:
	•	they already love each other
	•	they didn’t know
	•	they’re genuinely good people

So the whole movie becomes:

Can love survive a family reunion?

Feature Film Treatment

Genre: Romantic Comedy / Family Comedy
Tone: Meet the Parents meets My Big Fat Greek Wedding

⸻

Logline

After a year of blissful dating, a devoted couple attends a massive family reunion only to discover they are distant cousins. When they decide their love is worth fighting for, they must survive gossip, outrage, and a full family intervention before proving that sometimes love doesn’t follow the rules.

⸻

Tone & Style

We’re Cousins?!? is a warm, character-driven romantic comedy built around the chaos of extended family dynamics. The humor comes from awkward social situations, generational differences, and the absurd ways families react to uncomfortable truths.

The film balances laugh-out-loud ensemble comedy with heartfelt romance. While the premise is provocative, the story treats the relationship with sincerity and focuses on the emotional question:

Who gets to decide what love is allowed to be?

Visually, the film takes place largely during a vibrant summer family reunion filled with colorful shirts, smoky barbecue pits, kids playing games, and relatives catching up under strings of lights.

The reunion becomes the stage for escalating chaos.

⸻

ACT ONE

Jamie Carter is a warm, thoughtful elementary school teacher who prides herself on doing the right thing. Organized and responsible, Jamie tends to overthink everything in life—especially when it comes to her large, opinionated extended family.

Her boyfriend Marco Diaz is the opposite. Charming, easygoing, and naturally funny, Marco runs a popular food truck that has begun gaining a loyal following in their city. He lives life with optimism and believes most problems can be solved with honesty and conversation.

Jamie and Marco have been dating for nearly a year, and their relationship is easy, affectionate, and deeply supportive. They cook together, dance in their kitchen, and share a playful sense of humor. Friends often comment that they seem unusually perfect for each other.

The only thing missing is Marco meeting Jamie’s extended family.

Jamie’s mother Linda Carter calls with a reminder: the annual Carter Family Reunion is happening that weekend. It’s a massive event held every summer at a large public park where hundreds of relatives gather from across the state. Linda treats the reunion like a military operation, complete with color-coded schedules, matching family shirts, and an enormous printed Carter family tree displayed on a board for everyone to admire.

Jamie reluctantly invites Marco, warning him that her family can be “a lot.”

Marco is excited. He’s eager to finally meet the people who raised the woman he loves.

They arrive at the reunion park, where the atmosphere is lively and chaotic. Children play games in the grass, older relatives gossip in folding chairs, and the smell of barbecue fills the air. Jamie begins introducing Marco to relatives: aunts, uncles, cousins, and grandparents whose names Marco struggles to remember.

At first everything goes smoothly.

Until Marco notices something strange.

Many relatives mention last names he recognizes from his own family history. He laughs it off, assuming it’s coincidence.

Then he wanders over to the large family tree display.

There, on the board, he spots a name that stops him cold.

His grandmother’s.

Jamie joins him, and the two begin tracing branches of the tree with their fingers.

Slowly, the horrifying realization forms.

They share a common ancestor.

Jamie and Marco stare at each other in disbelief.

They are distant cousins.

⸻

ACT TWO

Panicked, the couple steps away from the reunion crowd to talk privately.

Jamie spirals into embarrassment and horror. She worries about what her family will say, what people will think, and whether their entire relationship has suddenly become unacceptable.

Marco takes a more practical view. They didn’t know. They are distant relatives, separated by several generations. From his perspective, nothing about their relationship has changed.

But before they can decide what to do, Aunt Denise, the family’s most enthusiastic gossip, overhears fragments of their conversation.

Within minutes the rumor spreads through the reunion like wildfire.

Relatives whisper. People stare.

The reactions are wildly different.

Uncle Ray loudly declares the relationship “wrong” and begins lecturing anyone who will listen. Cousin Tyrell pulls out his phone and starts researching cousin marriage laws online, reading them aloud like a news report. Jamie’s mother Linda panics about the embarrassment it could bring to the family.

Some relatives are shocked. Others are curious. A few find the whole situation strangely entertaining.

Soon Jamie and Marco are surrounded by relatives demanding explanations.

The reunion transforms into a chaotic intervention.

Marco tries to explain that their relation is distant and that they had no idea before today. Jamie, overwhelmed by the family’s reaction, begins to doubt the relationship she once felt so confident about.

The pressure intensifies. Family members debate genetics, morality, and tradition in increasingly ridiculous ways.

Marco insists their love should matter more than the opinions of distant relatives.

Jamie, however, fears that continuing the relationship could permanently fracture her family.

Their once effortless relationship begins to strain under the weight of everyone else’s expectations.

⸻

ACT THREE

Emotionally exhausted, Jamie retreats from the reunion and hides away near the quiet edge of the park.

There she encounters Great Grandmother Evelyn, the oldest member of the Carter family. Though physically frail, Evelyn has a sharp mind and a calm authority that commands respect.

She has been quietly observing the chaos all day.

Evelyn asks Jamie why she’s crying. Jamie confesses everything, admitting she feels ashamed and unsure whether loving Marco is now wrong.

Evelyn listens patiently.

Then she reveals a secret.

She and her late husband were also cousins.

When they met many decades earlier, such relationships were not uncommon in the small rural communities where they grew up. Their love was genuine, and they built a long, happy marriage together.

Evelyn gently tells Jamie that people often pretend love follows neat social rules—but real life is more complicated than that.

Encouraged by Evelyn’s wisdom, Jamie returns to the reunion just as the family gathers for the evening dinner.

Evelyn asks for everyone’s attention and tells her story publicly.

The room grows quiet.

Gradually the tension begins to dissolve. Relatives realize the situation may not be as shocking as they first believed. The laughter and warmth of the reunion slowly return.

Uncle Greg raises a barbecue fork like a toast.

“If they’re happy,” he says, “that’s good enough for me.”

One by one, the family softens.

Jamie and Marco finally stand together in front of everyone, no longer hiding their relationship.

The reunion concludes with a large family photo.

As the photographer counts down, someone jokes that next year they should double-check the family tree before anyone starts dating.

Everyone laughs.

Jamie and Marco stand together at the center of the photo, their relationship intact—and stronger for having survived the chaos.

The camera flashes.

⸻

Final Note

We’re Cousins?!? is a comedy about how families react when their expectations collide with real love. Beneath the outrageous premise is a heartfelt message:

Sometimes the people who know you best are the ones who need the most convincing.
