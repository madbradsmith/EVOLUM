INT. RESTAURANT KITCHEN - DAY
The Food Factory restaurant, one of 40 in an ever-expanding
chain of identical locales. Think Cheesecake Factory, except
without the cheesecake so nobody gets sued.
VERONICA BROOKS, 33, like a power-suited octopus with a
controlling hand in everything, sweeps through the kitchen
checking nervous EMPLOYEES and their prep.
VERONICA
Ten minutes until doors open,
people!
DING. She pauses just long enough to check and be annoyed by
an incoming text.
VERONICA (CONT'D)
Really right now?
ABE, a cook, carries a lexan pan filled with broccoli.
VERONICA (CONT'D)
Abe? We’ve gotten a few complaints
about the au gratin being soupy.
You drained that broccoli, right?
Abe doubles back to the sink with the broccoli he hadn’t
drained.
VERONICA (CONT'D)
(as she’s texting)
The permit is due by nine a.m.
tomorrow. You said you’d handle
this.
She checks a food warmer.
VERONICA (CONT'D)
Gilbert, this hollandaise is going
to break before the brunch crowd
even gets here.
Veronica flicks down the temperature knob before GILBERT can
get to it. Veronica pulls out her phone again, her fingers
flying.
VERONICA (CONT'D)
Email... housing permit... sent.
(as she’s texting)
I took care of it, Cornelius. Feel
free to sleep in late tomorrow.
2.
Back to it. She tastes sauces with demitasse spoons and
checks off boxes on her perfect clipboard.
JANET, old enough to stop being a hostess, rushes in,
cordless phone in hand.
JANET
Miss Brooks, you have a call on
line two.
VERONICA
In the middle of food check? Why is
that?
JANET
Because that’s when the phone
rang...?
VERONICA
But you brought me the phone in the
middle of -- Thanks, Janet. Take a
message. Please.
Janet slinks off.
VERONICA (CONT'D)
Why must I do everything?
(louder, almost sincere)
You’re doing a great job,
everybody.
A no look pass as Veronica exits the kitchen,
VERONICA (CONT'D)
Carla, watch those lardons.
The pan CARLA wasn’t watching flames up. Carla slides a lid
on just in time to avoid disaster.
DAVE, a chef who’s okay with his salary as a cook, tries just
a little too late to avoid Veronica’s path.
VERONICA (CONT'D)
Dave --
DAVE
Yep. Right here.
VERONICA
What do you think about adding a
little bit more cinnamon to the
pancetta waffles?
Dave shrugs.
2.
3.
DAVE
You actually asking for my culinary
expertise or are you telling me to
add more cinnamon?
Veronica shrugs.
DAVE (CONT'D)
Yep. More cinnamon, boss.
VERONICA
One day, when it’s called The Dave
Factory --
DAVE
Wouldn’t call it that. But go on.
